What broke

These are the assumptions I had to unlearn.

Determinism is gone
Same input, different output every time. You can't design one screen anymore. You're designing for a spectrum.
The interface isn't the product
The model is the product. We're designing a layer over something we don't fully control.
Failure looks like success
AI doesn't show a 404. It gives a confident wrong answer that looks perfect. Our users won't know it's wrong.
Context replaces navigation
Users don't click through pages. The AI holds the context. Flows, menus, wizards. Might not exist soon.

Where we are, where we're going

Four paradigms. Each one changes what designers do.

Where we are
Rule-based
"If someone says pricing, send this message." The business owner builds every path. AI does exactly what it's told.
Designer designs screens
Emerging
AI-assisted
AI suggests: "People who ask about pricing usually ask about shipping next." The human still decides. AI makes them faster.
Designer designs suggestions
Building now
Agentic
AI notices a customer asked about a product, checks inventory, personalizes the response, and sends it. At 3am. Without asking.
Designer designs the guardrails
Next
AI-native
User types: "Handle pricing questions for my store." AI generates the entire flow. The interface isn't a builder anymore. It's a prompt.
Designer designs the conversation

Generative AI powers all of this. It's the engine. These are the design paradigms we build around it.

What research is telling us

What I've learned from research, conferences, and building AI features.

Reviewing AI work is harder than doing it yourself
When AI does 80-90% correctly, catching subtle errors is more cognitively exhausting than starting from scratch. "AI draft + human review" may be the worst pattern.
Tankelevitch et al. · CHI 2024
Users prefer control over capability
Given a choice between a more capable autonomous AI and a less capable controllable one, users choose control. Even when it produces worse results.
Google DeepMind + PAIR 2024
Failure recovery beats accuracy
AI that fails 20% but recovers gracefully is trusted more than AI that fails 5% silently. Error handling matters more than error prevention.
Microsoft Research 2024
Fluency causes dangerous over-trust
The more human the AI sounds, the less users verify its outputs. Even when warned. Conversational fluency is a double-edged sword.
Bucinca et al. · CHI 2024

What's not being designed right now

These problems exist today at Manychat. Nobody owns them yet.

Recovery
If AI sends a wrong DM to 10,000 subscribers at 3am, what's our recovery path? There isn't one. We need to design it.
Conflict
When two automations contradict each other, what wins? We haven't decided. The AI will have to. Should it?
Surface
The flow editor is our entire design surface. What if users stop needing it? We need to know what we're building next.
Oversight
Who's reviewing what the AI actually says to end users? Not in staging. In production. Right now.
